About Anne Frank

Anne Frank (born June 12, 1929, Frankfurt am Main, Germany—died February/March 1945, Bergen-Belsen concentration camp, near Hannover) Jewish girl whose diary of her family’s two years in hiding during the German occupation of the Netherlands became a classic of war literature. Anne Frank was a Jewish girl who gained international fame posthumously after her diary, "The Diary of a Young Girl", was published. She documented her experiences hiding during the Nazi occupation of the Netherlands in World War II.

Key Facts

  • Anne Frank was born on June 12, 1929, in Frankfurt, Germany.
  • Her family fled to Amsterdam in 1933 to escape persecution from the Nazis.
  • Anne and her family went into hiding in 1942, spending over two years in a hidden annex.
  • Her diary, which she received as a birthday present, was published in 1947.
  • Anne Frank died of typhus in the Bergen-Belsen concentration camp in March 1945, just weeks before the camp was liberated.
